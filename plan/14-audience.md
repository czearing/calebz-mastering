# Audience

Design for one person. The site converts when it speaks to the artist who already cares.

## Who books this

The core client is an engaged, self-releasing artist whose music has a small active community and whose tracks could measurably sound better. They care intensely about how the release sounds and cannot finish it themselves.

| Trait | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Stage | Independent, self-releasing, no label or in-house engineer |
| Reach | Small but active. Real comments and reposts, not vanity counts |
| Genre | Electronic and adjacent, the work CalebZ masters well |
| Mindset | Cares about the release, wants polish, values a human ear |
| Spend | Will pay for the finished version once they trust the sound |

Secondary: managers and small labels who need consistency and turnaround. Returning clients who just want their delivery link.

## What they need to believe

1. He can make my track sound better. Proven by before and after.
2. He gets my genre. Proven by the selected work and language.
3. This is reliable. Proven by testimonials and a clear process.
4. Booking is easy and low risk. Proven by a short form and revisions.

## What they fear

- Generic mastering that flattens the character of the mix.
- A black box with no communication.
- Paying before hearing proof.
- A slow or confusing back and forth.

The site answers each fear directly: hear the work first, see the process, read real names, book in one screen.

## Language that lands

- Talk in their terms: loudness, translation, club versus phone, headroom.
- Name the genre, not "all styles".
- Show the work before asking for anything.
- Quote real artists with real names and projects.

## What to avoid

- Speaking to everyone. A site for everyone converts no one.
- Studio-jargon that excludes a self-taught producer.
- Claims with no proof attached next to them.
